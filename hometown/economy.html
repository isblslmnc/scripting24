The people in El Nido thrive on having businesses that provide services to tourists. When I went there with my family, we found out that it is cheaper to rent a motorcycle rather than commuting by tricycles. So, there are a lot of places where you can rent a motorcycle for as long as your stay there to use. Airbnb rentals are also quite common in El Nido for travelers’ accommodations. I also saw a lot of locals by the shore that are selling real pearls to tourists. We were told they were personally diving to get the pearls they were selling to us.

Other than its booming businesses that cater to tourists, El Nido also thrives on its fishing and agriculture industries. This sustains their local markets and is used for the food included in their island-hopping packages.
