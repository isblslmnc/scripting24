The town of El Nido serves as the main hub and is situated near Bacuit Bay. The Bacuit Archipelago, which includes over 40 islands and islets, lies to the northeast of the town, forming the region's most iconic geographical feature. This group of islands includes popular destinations like Miniloc Island, Matinloc Island, Shimizu Island, and Cadlao Island, as well as famous lagoons such as Big Lagoon and Small Lagoon.

The place is known for its stunning location which is surrounded by limestone cliffs, white sandy beaches, and crystal-clear turquoise waters. You will see a clear view of their beautiful landscape the moment that you land in Palawan, but the beaches are very visible at El Nido. Additionally, there are large, small, and even a secret lagoon that can only be accessed by squeezing through a small opening.

The area is also known for having multiple islands surrounding it, which makes island-hopping a popular activity. El Nido is also famous for having beautiful mangroves and coral reefs which are seen when scuba diving.
